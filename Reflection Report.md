**#Cybersecurity Reflection**



**1.What cybersecurity risks did you identify in your own digital habits?**

In my own digital habits, I identified several cybersecurity risks. One risk is using simple passwords that are easy to remember but may also be easy for attackers to guess. Another risk is reusing the same password for multiple accounts. If one account gets hacked, all other accounts using the same password could be compromised. I also realized that I sometimes click on links in emails or messages without carefully checking whether they are legitimate. Additionally, I do not always update my software immediately, which can leave my devices vulnerable to security threats.



**2.What security improvements will you implement immediately?**

To improve my security, I will implement several changes immediately. First, I will create strong and unique passwords for all my online accounts. These passwords will include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Second, I will enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible to add an extra layer of protection. Third, I will be more cautious when opening emails, clicking links, or downloading attachments from unknown sources. I will also keep my operating system, applications, and antivirus software updated to protect against newly discovered threats. Finally, I will regularly back up important files to prevent data loss.



**3.Why is cybersecurity important in today's world?**

Cybersecurity is extremely important in today's world because people rely heavily on the internet for communication, education, banking, shopping, and work. Cybercriminals are constantly looking for ways to steal personal information, money, and sensitive data. A successful cyberattack can cause financial loss, identity theft, privacy violations, and damage to businesses and individuals. By following good cybersecurity practices, people can protect their personal information, maintain privacy, and reduce the risk of becoming victims of cybercrime. Therefore, cybersecurity awareness and responsible online behavior are essential for everyone in the digital age.
