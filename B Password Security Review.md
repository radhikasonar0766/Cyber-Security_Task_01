1\. What Makes a Strong Password?

A strong password is one that is difficult for attackers to guess or crack using automated tools. It should be long, complex, and unique. The strength of a password depends on its length, randomness, and the variety of characters used.



Characteristics of a strong password:

* Contains at least 12–16 characters.
* Includes uppercase letters (A–Z) and lowercase letters (a–z).
* Contains numbers (0–9).
* Includes special characters (e.g., @, #, $, %, \&, \*).
* Does not contain personal information such as names, birthdays, phone numbers, or college names.
* Is not based on common words or predictable patterns.



Example of a strong password: M!ghtyRiver#2026$Sun

Strong passwords significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access and protect sensitive information from cybercriminals.



2\. Why Should Passwords Be Unique?

Each account should have its own unique password. Using a different password for every account ensures that if one account is compromised, the others remain secure.



Benefits of unique passwords:

* Prevents attackers from accessing multiple accounts with a single stolen password.
* Reduces the impact of data breaches.
* Improves overall personal and organizational security.
* Protects sensitive information stored across different platforms.



For example, if your social media account is hacked and you use the same password for your email account, attackers can easily gain access to both accounts.



3\. What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is a security method that requires users to provide two or more forms of verification before accessing an account or system.



MFA typically combines:

* Something you know – Password or PIN.
* Something you have – Mobile phone, authentication app, or security token.
* Something you are – Fingerprint, face recognition, or other biometric data.



Example: After entering your password, you receive a one-time verification code on your smartphone. Access is granted only after entering that code correctly.



Advantages of MFA:

* Provides an extra layer of security.
* Protects accounts even if passwords are stolen.
* Reduces the risk of phishing and credential theft.
* Helps organizations comply with security standards.



4\. What Are the Risks of Password Reuse?

Password reuse occurs when the same password is used for multiple accounts. This practice creates a major security risk.



Risks include:

* Credential Stuffing Attacks: Attackers use stolen usernames and passwords from one website to access other accounts.
* Multiple Account Compromise: A breach on one platform can lead to unauthorized access to several accounts.
* Identity Theft: Attackers may gain access to personal and financial information.
* Data Loss: Sensitive files, emails, and documents may be exposed or deleted.
* Financial Fraud: Banking, shopping, or payment accounts may be targeted.



For example, if a student's password is leaked from a gaming website and the same password is used for college and email accounts, attackers could gain access to academic records and personal information.



Password Policy



Purpose

The purpose of this password policy is to protect user accounts, sensitive information, and organizational systems from unauthorized access.



Password Best Practices

1. Use at Least 12 Characters: Passwords should be a minimum of 12 characters long. Longer passwords are harder to crack using automated tools.
2. Use Uppercase and Lowercase Letters: Combine both letter cases to increase password complexity and security.
3. Include Numbers and Special Characters: Use a mixture of letters, numbers, and symbols to make passwords more resistant to guessing attacks.
4. Avoid Personal Information: Do not use names, birthdays, phone numbers, student IDs, or easily discoverable information.
5. Use a Unique Password for Every Account: Never reuse passwords across multiple websites or services.
6. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Whenever Possible: Add an extra verification step to protect accounts even if passwords are compromised.
7. Do Not Share Passwords: Passwords should remain confidential and should never be shared with friends, colleagues, or family members.
8. Use a Password Manager: Store and generate strong passwords securely using a trusted password manager.
9. Change Compromised Passwords Immediately :If a password is suspected to be exposed, update it without delay.
10. Avoid Writing Passwords in Visible Locations: Do not write passwords on sticky notes, notebooks, or unsecured files.
